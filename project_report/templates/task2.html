Analyses via graphics

On this page you can see graphs that reflect the distribution of a particular parameter. In the next three paragraphs, a description of each graph will be presented.

The first graph is a bar chart displaying information about the types of sore throats.

TA: Typical Angina

ATA: Atypical Angina

NAP: Non-Anginal Pain

ASY: Asymptomatic

We can notice that most of it is due to the asymptomatic type of the disease. This is followed by non-anginal pain. The remaining part falls on typical and atypical angina.

Speaking of the following diagram, it should be noted that its legend can be seen when you hover over it. Looking at the pie chart itself, it can be seen that among all respondents, approximately 45% suffered a heart attack, but 55% did not encounter this disease, given that 100% of respondents felt heart pain.

Now let's talk about another pie chart, which contains information about the gender of respondents. We can notice that only 21% of women, while 79% of men. Thus, it can be said for sure that men are at greater risk than women. We will try to find out further patterns when considering other graphs.
